Abstract

Text-to-scenario generation for social navigation in semantically grounded simulation worlds.

Social navigation is critical for robots in human environments, yet most simulators evaluate methods in static or oversimplified settings. We propose a text-to-dynamic scenario generator that compiles natural-language prompts into interactive simulation worlds with time-evolving environments and controllable human behaviors. Prompts are transformed into parameterized scenario programs specifying layouts, roles, intents, motion styles, interaction goals, temporal schedules, and exogenous events. Human activity is instantiated via probabilistic, time-varying parameters (e.g., enter → queue → order → sit/move) using behavior trees coupled with LLM-based reasoning and a curated database of behavior models. These behaviors are grounded in semantically annotated zones and regions of interest (e.g., doors, counters, seats), constraining where agents move and which actions they can perform for realistic interaction. To ensure simulator independence, we introduce a unified pedestrian-state wrapper that abstracts over commonly used simulators. Using this approach, we generate realistic social scenarios directly integrable into Gazebo, Isaac Sim, and Unity, and evaluate their realism and usability through commonly utilized metrics and user studies.

Method Overview

Key Ideas

End-to-end pipeline that transforms natural-language prompts into executable interactive worlds and scenarios.

Semantic world representation (world.yaml) with ROIs/POIs to ground roles, intents, and behaviors.

Unified ROS 2 pedestrian-state wrapper for portability across Gazebo, Isaac Sim, and Unity.

Behavior trees + LLM/RAG reasoning with probabilistic, time-varying parameters for realistic human activity.
